Ireland's Impact On Life Below Water

Introduction

In September 2015, Ireland and all the other United Nation Member States agreed to follow an agenda called the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development ("Transforming our World"). This agenda was about the 17 Development Goals and their 169 targets. 10 of these targets are for Life Under Water.

Ireland has loads of maratime animals. Flip the picture of them to find out more about them!

Fun Fact!

In 1991 Irish waters were named Europe's first whale and dolphin sanctuary. Minkes, Fin Whales, Humpbacks, Killer Whales all live in waters around Ireland!

What did Ireland Promise

Ireland promised to:

  • Reduce marine pollution
  • Manage and protect marine and coastal ecosystems to avoid negatively impacting them
  • Reduce the impact of ocean acidification
  • Regulate harvesting and end overfishing
  • Conserve at least 10% of coastal and marine areas
  • Prohibit certain forms of fisheries subsidies which worsen overfishing and to get rid of subsidies that contribute to illegal unregulated fishing
  • Increase the economic benefits to Small Island Developing States and least developed countries from the sustainable use of marine resources
  • Increase scientific knowledge around life below water
  • Provide access for small-scale artisanal fishers to marine resources and markets
  • Improve the protection and sustainable use of oceans and their resources by implementing international law as explained in UNCLOS
Fun Fact!

The two types of seals found in Ireland are Grey Seals and Common Seals. They both live about 25-35 years.

What Progress has been made?

Ireland is ranked 8th out of 12* in terms of this SDG. This may in part be due to limited data availability for measuring the progress that has been made towards it. Some recommendations have been given to help improve Ireland's efforts towards the SDGs. These were split into two parts, things to do on a national level and things to do on a local level:

National:

  • The National Integrated Maritime Plan needs to be fully implemented
  • Regulate harvesting and end over-fishing
  • Implement policies to help fishing stocks to recover to sustainable levels.

Local:

  • Put a plan in place to tackle pesticides in drinking water
  • Implement the 'nature' programs that are in the Climate Action Plan published by the Department of Communications, Climate Action and the Environment
*The data was compared using 3 indicators over 12 countries

Why is it important that Ireland does its part?

It is important that Ireland does it part in working towards this sustainability goal as the seas around Ireland are some of the most productive and biologically sensitive areas in EU waters. Fishing is important to Ireland both socially and economically. For thousands of years Irelands coastline has provided seafood for us and it’s important that we protect it for future generations.

Fun Fact!

Bottlenose Dolphins, Harbour Porpoises, Risso's Dolphins and Common Dolphins can be found in Irish waters. Sometimes they go up to boats to see what’s happening!
